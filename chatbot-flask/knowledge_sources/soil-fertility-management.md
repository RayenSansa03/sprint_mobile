# Soil Fertility Management

## Core approach
- Start with a soil test before any fertilization program.
- Correct pH first, then design nutrient inputs.
- Combine organic matter and targeted mineral fertilization.

## Nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium
- Nitrogen supports vegetative growth but excess delays maturity.
- Phosphorus supports rooting and early development.
- Potassium supports stress tolerance and fruit quality.

## Field actions
- Rotate crops to reduce nutrient depletion and disease pressure.
- Introduce compost and crop residues to improve structure.
- Split applications instead of one heavy dose.
- Monitor leaf color and growth weekly for correction.
